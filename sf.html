Secondary Research

Women frequently feel uncomfortable or unsafe while traveling in New York. The harsh reality they have to face is that they are vulnerable to harassment or assault, whether as a pedestrian, by public transport, or when using rideshare apps and taxis.

Attempting to "Travel Safely":

  1. Keep phones fully charged or bring backup power sources
  2. Go into public establishments when you‚Äôre being followed
  3. Call someone and keep them on the phone when walking alone, share location with multiple emergency contacts
  4. Stick to well-lit areas and avoid ‚Äúhigh crime‚Äù neighborhoods
  5. Stay in crowded areas
  6. Take self-defense courses
  7. Take a taxi or rideshare at night
  8. Always travel in groups and don‚Äôt go out after midnight
  9. Scream if you‚Äôre attacked
These are the steps drilled into women -- whether by teachers, parents, [opinion pieces], the [MTA‚Äôs official guidelines], they are always expected to protect themselves and be on guard.

Interpersonal Solution

Buddy System App

Many of our users said they feel safer when there are people around or prefer to travel in groups. One solution is to create an app that matches women traveling on similar routes through the city.

Pros

  • Gives users a sense of security as they would feel safer traveling through the city with another person
  • Companion present who is ‚Äúresponsible‚Äù for your safety (and you for theirs). Instead of relying on bystanders in passing, this would give you one other person who you could rely on during transportation.
    • Users would not have use technology while out, instead other person is physically present. This allows the user to be more aware of their surroundings rather than having to be active on an app or phone call during travel.

Cons

  • ‚ÄúBuddy‚Äù traveling with may not have the correct intentions. How would you filter people on the app? Would there have to be an application process with background checks?
  • Matches would not always be entirely perfect. Users may still have to walk an extra block alone.
  • Places burden of safety on users. Instead of fixing the challenge to a user‚Äôs safety (people who want to cause harm) this makes users responsible for their own safety.
  • While making people ‚Äúfeel safe‚Äù it does not guarantee safety. It is possible to still be assaulted in a group. Traveling with one other person does entirely prevent assaults.

User Feedback

  • One user did not like this design solution because it requires that she must plan in advance. She would already be out when she begins to feel unsafe so this would not be helpful.
  • User was concerned about the safety implications, specifically: being paired with a stranger when alone, having to walk to a meet point, distraction looking at phone.
  • One user said they could achieve a similar effect by calling people they feel closer to.

Community Solution

Safe Havens & Bystander Intervention

  • Safe Havens: Establish a network of clearly-marked shops, pharmacies, etc. which will shelter people who feel threatened
  • Bystander Intervention: Provide training so bystanders can prevent, de-escalate, or report cases of Harassment and Assault

Pros

  • These programs de-center individual responsibility, and instead provide a network of care to protect potential victims. Safe Havens and bystanders can remove women from threatening situations, halting potential emergencies.

Cons

  • These programs de-center individual responsibility, and instead provide a network of care to protect potential victims. Safe Havens and bystanders can remove women from threatening situations, halting potential emergencies.
